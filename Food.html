Iranian cuisine is known for its rich flavors and diverse dishes. Here are some traditional Iranian foods:

1. Kebabs: Iranians love their kebabs, which can be made from various meats such as lamb, chicken, or beef, and often served with rice and grilled tomatoes.
2. Tahdig: This is a beloved Persian dish, made by crisping the bottom layer of rice in the pot, creating a delicious crunchy texture. It's often served as a side dish.
3. Ghormeh Sabzi: A popular Iranian stew made with a mixture of fresh herbs, red beans, and tender chunks of meat (usually beef or lamb).
4. Dolmeh: Stuffed vegetables, like grape leaves or bell peppers, filled with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes ground meat, cooked in a savory tomato sauce.
5. Fesenjan: A unique dish made from pomegranate paste and crushed walnuts, often served with chicken or duck. It has a sweet and tangy flavor.
6. Zereshk Polo: A colorful rice dish made with barberries, saffron, and sometimes chicken. The tartness of the barberries balances the saffron's sweetness.
7. Ash Reshteh: A hearty noodle soup filled with beans, herbs, and sometimes meat, traditionally eaten during the Persian New Year.
8. Mirza Ghasemi: A flavorful eggplant and tomato dish, often served as a dip with bread. It's made with garlic, eggs, and spices.
9. Abgoosht: Also known as "Dizi," this is a traditional Iranian stew made with lamb or beef, chickpeas, white beans, and various spices. It's often eaten in two stages: first as a soup, then the remaining ingredients are mashed together and eaten with bread.
10. Halva: A popular dessert made from ingredients like sesame paste, wheat flour, or rice, sweetened with sugar or honey and flavored with cardamom or rosewater.
11. Baklava: Although associated with various Middle Eastern cuisines, Iranians also enjoy this sweet pastry made from layers of filo dough, nuts, and sweet syrup.
12. Saffron Rice: Saffron is a prized spice in Iranian cuisine and is often used to flavor rice, giving it a beautiful golden color and a subtle, fragrant taste.
These are just a few examples of the many delicious dishes you can find in Iranian cuisine, which reflects the country's rich culinary history and regional diversity.
